### Skills
I find the biggest skill to have at this point in time is just to ask questions. We've been learning our tools all year, and if something isn't working, there's a problem that could be solved the easy way, which is to show chatgpt and tell it to give you the answers. But you don't have to make it so excessively hard on yourself where you're redoing all of your learning from scratch. If you took notes, check your notes, and then ask questions on slack. I showed this as I asked many questions to people who have experience in it, many of which are my friends from college.

Another skill I have shown since the last blog entry is perseverance. Perseverance is a hard one to show since it's really all about pushing yourself when you wanna quit. However, I think I've shown it quite well as I felt like giving up on using node many times, and I've also felt like I didn't give up on my code, and I'd gotten what I was working on to work. This did require me to ask lots of questions because if I hadn't figured it out, I would have given up.
